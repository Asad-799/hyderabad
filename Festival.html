1. Bathukamma is a vibrant nine-day floral festival celebrated mainly by women in Telangana, symbolizing the “Mother Goddess come alive” and honoring nature’s abundance during the monsoon season.
  2. Women create beautiful stacks of local seasonal flowers, sing traditional songs, and dance in circles around the floral arrangements, fostering community and celebrating femininity
  3. The festival concludes with the immersion of Bathukamma flower stacks in water bodies, marking the beginning of autumn and reinforcing the bond between people, earth, and water
  1. Bonalu is a traditional Telangana festival dedicated to Goddess Mahakali, celebrated as a thanksgiving for protection from diseases and calamities, especially originating from a 19th-century plague in Hyderabad.
  2. Women prepare a special meal called Bonam (rice cooked with jaggery and milk) placed in decorated pots, which they carry on their heads to temples as an offering to the goddess.
  3. The festival features vibrant processions, folk dances, and rituals symbolizing devotion, community unity, and the power of the feminine divine.
